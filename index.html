The Full Story

A Timeline of Broken Promises

Every step of this journey revealed a pattern — a brand that sells premium experiences but delivers something far beneath that promise.

🛒
1 FEBRUARY 2026
The Order — Full of Optimism

We placed an order for a handcrafted temple unit. WoodenStreet's website promised premium quality, customisation, and delivery within 20 days. The price reflected all of that. We paid in full, excited to welcome a beautiful piece into our home.

📵
DAYS 21–40 — RADIO SILENCE
A 20-Day Promise Turned Into 40 Days — With No Word From Them

The 20-day window came and went. No message. No email. No call. We were the ones reaching out — repeatedly — to find out where our order was. WoodenStreet's response? Vague assurances and shifting timelines. A basic courtesy — a heads-up about the delay — was entirely absent.

📦
40 DAYS LATER — DELIVERY
It Finally Arrived — And It Was Not What We Ordered

The temple was delivered, but what arrived bore little resemblance to what was promised. The wood had a yellowed, aged appearance — not the rich, warm tones shown online. There were visible patches across the surface, chips in the wood, and the finish looked inconsistent. This was not premium craftsmanship.

🔧
POST-DELIVERY — ASSEMBLY FAILURE
The Structure Couldn't Even Stand Steadily

Once assembled, the problems multiplied. The entire structure wobbled — an unnerving instability for something meant to hold sacred items. The drawer channel was defective, making the drawer almost impossible to open or close. This wasn't wear and tear — it was a manufacturing failure right out of the box.

👷
WEEKS OF FOLLOW-UP
Three Carpenters. Zero Solutions. We Had to Call Each Time.

WoodenStreet sent three different carpenters across multiple visits. Not one of them could restore the piece to an acceptable condition. What made it worse — we had to initiate every visit ourselves. WoodenStreet never once followed up proactively. We were left chasing a resolution that never came.

💳
THE RESOLUTION (OR LACK THEREOF)
No Refund. Just a Credit Note — And Then More Waiting.

After exhausting every avenue for a repair, WoodenStreet agreed to take the item back — but refused to issue a refund. Their offer: a credit note, forcing us to spend more money with the same brand that had failed us. Under protest, we accepted. That was over 10 days ago. The product has still not been picked up. We're waiting, again, with no update.

Quality Breakdown

Six Failures in One Order

This wasn't a single isolated issue — it was a systematic collapse across delivery, quality, and customer service.

📅
100% Delivery Delay

Promised in 20 days. Arrived in 40. No proactive communication about the delay at any point during the wait.

🪵
Substandard Wood Quality

Yellowed discolouration, surface patches, and visible chips — inconsistent with premium product standards advertised online.

⚖️
Structural Instability

The assembled unit wobbled noticeably. A piece holding religious items cannot be unsafe — this was a fundamental build failure.

🗄️
Faulty Drawer Mechanism

The drawer channel was defective from day one. It could not be opened or closed smoothly, even after multiple carpenter visits.

📞
No Proactive Support

Three repair attempts. Zero callbacks initiated by WoodenStreet. Every single follow-up had to be driven entirely by the customer.

🔄
Refund Refused

Despite a clear product defect, a cash refund was denied. Only a credit note was offered — and 10+ days later, the pickup is still pending.

"We didn't just receive a defective product — we received indifference. A brand that demands premium prices should stand behind its work with premium accountability."
